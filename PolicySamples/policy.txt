   <p style="margin:0in;margin-left:.375in;font-family:Calibri;font-size:11.0pt"><span style="font-weight:bold">Definition</span></p>
   <ul type="circle" style="margin-left:.75in;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:embed;
    margin-top:0in;margin-bottom:0in">
    <li style="margin-top:0;margin-bottom:0;vertical-align:middle;color:black"><span style="font-family:Calibri;font-size:11.0pt">The collection development
        policy provides information to the library's stakeholders about how the
        collection is chosen, and it explains who is responsible for making
        decisions about the collection.</span></li>
   </ul>
   <ul type="circle" style="margin-left:.75in;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:embed;
    margin-top:0in;margin-bottom:0in">
    <li style="margin-top:0;margin-bottom:0;vertical-align:middle"><span style="font-family:Calibri;font-size:11.0pt">The purpose of a
        collection development policy is to create a collection of library
        materials that supports the library's mission</span></li>
   </ul>
   <ul type="circle" style="margin-left:.75in;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:embed;
    margin-top:0in;margin-bottom:0in">
    <li style="margin-top:0;margin-bottom:0;vertical-align:middle"><span style="font-family:Calibri;font-size:11.0pt">Your collection
        development policy provides information for people who are interested
        in the library's collection. These people include:</span></li>
    <ul type="disc" style="margin-left:.375in;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:embed;
     margin-top:0in;margin-bottom:0in">
     <li style="margin-top:0;margin-bottom:0;vertical-align:middle"><span style="font-family:Calibri;font-size:11.0pt">the staff who have
         responsibility for selecting and maintaining the collection under the
         guidelines set by the governing body, </span></li>
     <li style="margin-top:0;margin-bottom:0;vertical-align:middle"><span style="font-family:Calibri;font-size:11.0pt">members of the public who
         may want to know why certain material is or is not included in the
         collection,</span></li>
    </ul>
    <ul type="disc" style="margin-left:.375in;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:embed;
     margin-top:0in;margin-bottom:0in">
     <li style="margin-top:0;margin-bottom:0;vertical-align:middle"><span style="font-family:Calibri;font-size:11.0pt">and the library's
         governing body (e.g. public library board, school board), which wants
         to have a consistent position regarding the library's collection.</span></li>
    </ul>
    <ul type="disc" style="margin-left:.375in;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:embed;
     margin-top:0in;margin-bottom:0in">
     <li style="margin-top:0;margin-bottom:0;vertical-align:middle"><span style="font-family:Calibri;font-size:11.0pt">One important function of
         the collection development policy is to state the library's position
         on intellectual freedom and to describe the library's policy and
         procedures for dealing with complaints about its materials.</span></li>
    </ul>
   </ul>
